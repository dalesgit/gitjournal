# Trinity 2

over the years I have preached from basically two different perspectives. For a number of years as I was first getting my feet wet, I would try to follow in the steps of the great teachers of the past. I had spent a semester at Notre Dame with one of the great English teachers on the Trinity, Catherine Lacunia. it was one of the most difficult Seminars that I ever had. Each day I worked really hard to try to understand the words coming from her mouth. Some Days I did better than others. I called the First perspective intellectualizing.

Even Before I became self-aware of the problems with talking about the Trinity, from an intellectual perspective, I had an interior feeling that trying to know, and understand, certainly the Trinity, but also many other things in life, was only half the truth.

If the danger of preaching on Trinity Sunday was to teach heresy, that is contrasted with orthodoxy. From my confirmation days as a teenager, I spent a lot of time worrying about orthodoxy. But gradually, I began to understand that there was a second perspective. It had to do with Doing. Christian faith is only one part about the intellectual understanding. and even older perspective focuses on doing. There was an old theological term aimed at that perspective and contrasting with orthodoxy. Orthopraxis. In the earliest days of the church, for centuries, in fact, converts to the faith were more likely to see what other Christians were like, what their lives were like, and to conclude that "I want to be like them."
