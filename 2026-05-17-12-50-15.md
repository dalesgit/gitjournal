Dear Elizabeth, ... well right of the bat I don’t know how to address you so I will just proceed with the way I would if I were there in your presence. I realize that my voice is but a very small one in the midst of a multitude of presences that are calling on you in the light of your election yesterday. 

I was the rector of St. Mary’s Church on King St from 2001 until 2012. I knew several of your predecessors and even more colleagues who had served at one time or another at Holy Nativity. I retired in 2012 and began to teach at Chaminade University. So I am one of those retired clergy who is still a part of your Diocese. 

I had considered traveling to Hawaii in order to be present at the convention yesterday. I had been on the search committee that produced Bob. When I moved back to the mainland in 2014, I did so for personal and practical reasons, but I felt like I was leaving home. I currently live in the Tampa area of Florida, but Hawaii still feels like home and I have done my best to come to terms with the fact that much of my adult life I have felt disconnected from home. Originally Home had been Colorado and was so until the time I went off to seminary. Then in the fall of 2001 I had an experience that was both awesome and unexpected when I realized that Hawaii was in a deep sense my true Home.

All of this is by way of doing a smattering of introducing myself to you. I extended my warm best wishes and Aloha as you move toward your ordination and the beginning of your new ministry in the islands.
