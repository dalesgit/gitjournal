"If you want to build a ship, don't summon people to buy wood, prepare tools, distribute jobs, and organize the work, rather teach people the yearning for the wide, boundless ocean."
Antoine de Saint-Exupery

This from **Shift**  by Mark E Tidsworth.

The text ends with a quote from John O’Donoghue a blessing for longing. 

It is so dated, published in 2015. The text was recommended by the new (Bishop) in South Carolina. It’s strange that it feels like a message from outer space in the Diocese Of Southwest Florida.

He recognizes the need to move that is shift from what it was to be a church in the 20th century to a very different kind of place in the 21st. Shane Cleburne takes a prominent role.

The three questions that moved him from being a “Dunn“

1. what does it mean to be a disciple of Jesus Christ?
2. What does it mean to be a gathered community of disciples?
3. What does it mean to be a gathered community of disciples who join God on mission in the world?
